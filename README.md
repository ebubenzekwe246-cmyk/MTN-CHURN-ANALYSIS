📊 MTN Customer Churn Analysis

The project uses SQL Excel and Power BI to study how MTN customers stop using their services. The project aims to discover the main reasons behind customer departures which will help us safeguard our revenue base of ₦199M. The dashboard displays information for analysis. The main goal of our research is to identify customer departure reasons through analysis of their experience with our services. The research will show which customer groups and their subscription choices will result in increased customer turnover. The research will deliver practical solutions which will help to prevent revenue losses that occurred during the first quarter of the year. The research investigates two different aspects of customer loyalty.

The "Loyalty Breach" (Tenure vs. Churn) The data shows an Average Tenure of 31 months. The users who left the network after 2.5 years of usage showed) that they found the staying cost of the service more expensive than the value of their brand loyalty. The Total Revenue by Month chart reveals a crisis. The revenue declined from ₦80 million in February to ₦50 million in March which resulted in a 37 percent revenue decline within 30 days. The network outages during that time frame were the main cause for customers to stop using our services because our opponent ran aggressive marketing campaigns.

The 1.5GB Yearly plan stands as the most popular option yet customers exit our service because High Call Tariffs and Costly Data serve as the main reasons for breaking their contracts. Customers make long-term financial commitments yet they perceive daily connection charges as "nickeled and dimed" expenses that accumulate over time. The system fails to achieve gender neutrality because it terminates customer service when customers choose  to switch from one gender identity to another. The company experiences equal customer departure rates which split between its Male and Female customer base. The research shows that the problem affects all customer types and consumption patterns across various activities which need to be examined.
The Emergency Win-Back Campaign needs to start right away to recover users who left during March. The "Welcome Back" package provides customers with a three-month tariff discount that helps the business recover from declining revenue. 

CONCLUSION
The company should establish a Tenure-Based Loyalty Tier. The company should establish "Gold Member" status for users who maintain their membership beyond 24 months because their average tenure lasts 31 months. The system should provide members automatic "Data Top-ups" when their data bundles reach low levels to resolve the "Fast Data Exhaustion" issue. 
The 30 to 50 age group represents our most engaged customer segment who also face the highest danger of leaving. The company should develop Family/Business Hybrid Plans that enable customers to share data between multiple lines. The company can raise customer retention through the "household plan" which consolidates multiple users under one account. 
The data shows that our organization is losing its main support group. The company can stop revenue loss and decrease customer churn to stable single-digit rates by shifting its efforts from new customer acquisition towards protecting existing customers who have stayed for 31 months.
